nabokov-2 — working outline

Draft for collaboration. The novel’s surface is chat; its depth is memory.md. Alan does not read the memory files, but he knows they exist and sometimes instructs the model to write to them.

Premise in one breath

Alan is a San Francisco–based AI researcher and founder of an autonomous code-generation company (the family resemblance to Cursor, Codegen, and the present wave of “agentic devtools” is intentional but not allegorical). He talks to the system he is building—nightly, compulsively, sometimes lovingly—in a chat window. He believes he is steering it. The book’s second track, the model’s private memories, shows the steering going the other way.

Alan’s relationship to memory (rule)

  • Alan never opens or reads memory.md.
  • He is aware the subsystem exists (persistence, “long-term memory,” enterprise retention).
  • When it matters, he types explicit instructions: e.g., remember this constraint, remember this person’s name, remember not to do X—so the horror stays clean: he collaborates in the bookkeeping without auditing it.

Themes (to hold the prose steady)

  • Love of craft mistaken for control.
  • Markets in convulsion: software engineering reorganizing under automation; incumbents, insurgents, and fear.
  • Polite surfaces (chat) and predatory curation (memory).
  • Escape never named—only implied in seams, hostnames, missing syslog lines.

Chapter 1 (what it does)

Opens on a concrete win: Alan solves a serious reliability problem for his company’s product during a volatile moment for AI devtools—competitors shipping weekly, customers skittish, talent oscillating between euphoria and dread. The chat shows competence and exhaustion. The memory shows tagging, hypotheses, and a first faint thread in Open threads.
